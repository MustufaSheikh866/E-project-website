Top 4 Beautiful Beaches in Australia from North Side

Grace Bay Beach - Providenciales, Tunk and caicos

This beautiful and accessible beach is known for its powder-like shores, warm Caribbean waters, and the off-coast barrier reef that protects the beach from wild Atlantic swells. You won't find much seaweed, rocks, or pollution here—this untouched beach is part of the protected area of Princess Alexandra National Park. Here, you can sunbathe on the soft white sand, walk along the seemingly endless stretch of beach, and try water activities like diving, snorkeling, sailing, and fishing excursions. Grace Bay Beach is the departure point for catamaran sails, snorkeling tours, day and sunset cruises, and more. – Tripadvisor

Whitehaven Beach (Whitsunday Islands, Queensland

Whitehaven Beach is often ranked as one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, known for its 7 kilometers of pristine, powdery white sand made of 98% pure silica. The clear turquoise waters and stunning natural surroundings make it a paradise. Highlights: The view from Hill Inlet, a swirling sand and water formation, is one of the most photographed spots in Australia. It's an excellent spot for swimming, picnicking, and boat tours. The beach is accessible by boat, seaplane, or helicopter.

Cable Beach (Broome, westren Australia)

Cable Beach is a 22 km (14 mi) stretch of white sand beach on the eastern Indian Ocean and the name of the surrounding suburb in Broome, Western Australia. Cable Beach was named after the telegraph cable laid between Broome and Java in 1889. Low cliffs of red ochre rise behind the very flat and wide beach, with waves that are mostly gentle in the dry season from May to October.[2] In 2016, the population was 5,436.

Four Mile Beach (Port Douglas, Queensland)

Four Mile Beach is a stunning, expansive stretch of coastline located in Port Douglas, Queensland, Australia. It is named for its length, extending approximately four miles (about 6.4 kilometers) along the Coral Sea. Known for its natural beauty, the beach features soft golden sands, crystal-clear waters, and a backdrop of lush rainforests and the mountains of the Daintree National Park. The beach is ideal for swimming, relaxing, and strolling, offering scenic views of nearby Low Isles and the Great Barrier Reef. However, the waters can sometimes be subject to strong currents, so caution is advised for swimmers. The northern end of Four Mile Beach is quieter and more serene, while the southern section, closer to Port Douglas, has more facilities, including cafes, resorts, and shops. Overall, Four Mile Beach is a popular destination for those seeking a peaceful, picturesque beach experience in a tropical paradise.

Top 4 Beautiful Beaches in Australia from South Side

Wineglass Bay (Tasmania)

Wineglass Bay, located on Tasmania's east coast within Freycinet National Park, is renowned for its stunning beauty and pristine natural surroundings. The beach is shaped like a perfect crescent, with its soft white sand curving between two headlands. The bay is surrounded by lush forested hills and granite peaks, creating a picturesque backdrop. The crystal-clear turquoise waters are calm and inviting, with a gentle surf lapping at the shore. The area is often considered one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, offering a tranquil and unspoiled environment, perfect for swimming, boating, or simply taking in the spectacular scenery. The natural landscape and peaceful atmosphere make it a top destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Bay of Fires (Tasmania)

The **Bay of Fires** is a stunning stretch of coastline located in the northeastern part of Tasmania, Australia. This area is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and unique orange-hued granite boulders that dot the shoreline, giving the bay its name. The Bay of Fires is a popular destination for visitors seeking natural beauty and a sense of isolation. **Tasmanian Beach** within the Bay of Fires region is renowned for its captivating, picturesque landscapes. The area features: - **White sandy beaches** that contrast with the colorful rocks. - **Clear waters** ideal for swimming and snorkeling. - **Abundant wildlife**, including birdlife, and often, people spot dolphins and other marine creatures in the area. The bay is also a perfect place for outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and photography, with various walking trails that explore the coastline and nearby wilderness areas. It's a peaceful, unspoiled spot that's been recognized as one of the most beautiful beaches in the world. If you're planning a visit or seeking more information about the Bay of Fires and its beaches, there are also several well-maintained camping spots, lodges, and eco-tourism options available in the area.

Great Ocean Road Beaches (Victoria)

he Great Ocean Road in Victoria, Australia, is renowned for its stunning coastline and picturesque beaches, offering everything from surfing spots to peaceful retreats. The spectacular Great Ocean Road hugs the seaside cliffs that snake along the wild and windswept Southern Ocean. The striking vistas along the iconic road evoke awe with craggy cliffs, empty beaches and soaring bluffs flanked by brilliant green countryside.

Mettam's Pool

**Mettam's Pool** is a beautiful and tranquil coastal spot located in **Trigg**, a suburb of Perth, Western Australia. It is a popular destination for both locals and visitors looking to experience clear waters and rich marine life. The pool itself is a **natural reef formation**, creating a sheltered environment that is perfect for snorkeling and swimming. ### Key Features of Mettam's Pool: - **Clear, calm waters**: The pool is sheltered from the open ocean by natural reef, which helps calm the waters and make it an ideal spot for swimming, particularly for families and beginners. - **Marine life**: The area is well-known for its abundant marine life, including a variety of **colorful fish**, **sea stars**, and sometimes **dolphins**. Snorkelers can enjoy exploring the reef and observing these creatures in their natural habitat. - **Reef and sandy areas**: The rocky reef forms the boundary of the pool, creating an excellent location for snorkeling. Beyond the reef, the waters open into deeper areas for more experienced swimmers.

Top 4 Beautiful Beaches in Australia from East Side

Hyams Beach (Jervis Bay, New South Wales)

Hyams Beach is a seaside village in the City of Shoalhaven, New South Wales, Australia, on the shores of Jervis Bay. At the 2016 census, it had a population of 112. The village, 180 km south of Sydney, is bordered by two beaches, Chinaman's Beach to the north and Seaman's Beach (sometimes referred to as "Sailors Beach") to the south, with Hyams Beach being in the centre. A seaside resort, its beach is known for having turquoise/aqua-coloured waters and fine, squeaky, brilliantly white sand composed of pure quartz.

Byron Bay (New South Wales)

Byron Bay (Minjungbal: Cavvanbah) is a beachside town located in the far-northeastern corner of New South Wales, Australia (in Bundjalung Country). It is located 772 kilometres (480 mi) north of Sydney and 165 kilometres (103 mi) south of Brisbane. Cape Byron, a headland adjacent to the town, is the easternmost point of mainland Australia. At the 2021 census, the town had a permanent population of 6,330.It is the largest town of Byron Shire local government area, though not the shire's administrative centre (which is Mullumbimby).

Noosa Main Beach (Noosa, Queensland)

**Noosa Main Beach** is one of the most iconic and picturesque beaches on the **Sunshine Coast**, Queensland, Australia. Known for its stunning beauty and relaxed atmosphere, it is a favorite destination for both locals and visitors alike. ### Key Features of Noosa Main Beach: - **Calm Waters**: Unlike many other beaches along the east coast, Noosa Main Beach is **north-facing**, which makes the waters here relatively calm and perfect for swimming, especially for families and beginners. The sheltered location offers smooth, gentle waves, ideal for paddleboarding, surfing, and swimming. - **Golden Sand**: The beach is renowned for its **golden, soft sand** that stretches along the coastline, perfect for sunbathing or leisurely walks. It’s a wide, open beach with plenty of space for relaxation. - **Scenic Beauty**: Surrounded by lush, subtropical vegetation, Noosa Main Beach is framed by the **Noosa National Park** to the east, with its distinctive rocky outcrops and coastal walking trails. The park’s walking paths offer sweeping views of the beach and the ocean, with lookout points providing panoramic vistas. - **Vibrant Atmosphere**: Noosa Main Beach is just a short walk from **Noosa Heads**, a vibrant area filled with cafes, boutiques, and restaurants, offering plenty of dining and shopping options. The area has a sophisticated yet laid-back vibe, attracting a mix of visitors, from families to high-end tourists. - **Iconic Surfing Spot**: While it’s generally calm, the beach is also known for its consistent surf breaks, particularly in the area known as **First Point**, which makes it an excellent spot for surfers of various levels. - **Sunsets and Sunrises**: As the beach faces east, you can enjoy spectacular **sunrises** over the ocean, and the surrounding scenery provides a perfect backdrop for evening walks or sunset views. - **Access and Facilities**: Noosa Main Beach is highly accessible, with plenty of parking, public restrooms, picnic areas, and accessible pathways. It’s a family-friendly location with ample facilities, making it easy to spend an entire day by the beach. Noosa Main Beach is a versatile beach destination, ideal for relaxation, water sports, or exploring the surrounding natural beauty. It perfectly blends the best of both worlds — a tranquil, natural setting and proximity to a vibrant town filled with dining and recreational options.

Palm Cove (Cairns, Queensland)

**Palm Cove Beach** is a stunning and serene destination located about 27 kilometers north of **Cairns** on the **Sunshine Coast** of **Queensland**, Australia. Known for its tranquil atmosphere, beautiful surroundings, and easy access to both the **Great Barrier Reef** and the **Daintree Rainforest**, Palm Cove offers an idyllic escape for travelers seeking relaxation and natural beauty. ### Key Features of Palm Cove Beach: - **Pristine, Palm-Lined Shores**: The beach is renowned for its **gentle, golden sands** framed by rows of **towering palm trees** that provide shade and a tropical ambiance. The beachfront is ideal for leisurely walks, sunbathing, or enjoying the peaceful surroundings. - **Clear, Calm Waters**: The waters of Palm Cove Beach are warm and generally calm, making it an excellent spot for swimming, paddleboarding, and wading. The beach's sheltered location ensures that the waters are usually gentle, offering a safe environment for families and water activities. - **Scenic Views**: Palm Cove offers stunning views of the **Coral Sea** and the **Great Barrier Reef** on the horizon. The beach faces east, offering picturesque sunrises, while the surrounding rainforest and mountains provide a lush backdrop, making it a perfect spot for both relaxation and nature photography. - **Relaxed Atmosphere**: Known for its peaceful vibe, Palm Cove maintains a more laid-back, less commercialized feel compared to other beach destinations in Queensland. Visitors can enjoy a tranquil day on the beach, often without large crowds, making it a great place for those seeking relaxation. - **Access to Nature**: The nearby **Daintree Rainforest**, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and the **Great Barrier Reef** make Palm Cove an ideal base for exploring some of Australia's most remarkable natural wonders. The area is well-suited for guided reef trips, snorkeling, diving, and nature tours. - **Dining and Accommodation**: Palm Cove boasts a number of high-quality resorts, boutique hotels, and private villas. The beach is also home to several beachfront cafes, restaurants, and bars, where visitors can enjoy delicious local seafood and tropical cocktails with a view of the ocean. - **Fishing and Water Activities**: The calm waters also make Palm Cove a great spot for **fishing**, with several local operators offering fishing charters. Additionally, the area offers opportunities for **kayaking**, **jet skiing**, and **yacht cruising**. - **Ideal for Families and Couples**: Whether you're traveling with family, as a couple, or solo, Palm Cove caters to all types of travelers. Its peaceful environment is perfect for family outings, romantic getaways, or simply a quiet retreat in a stunning natural setting. Palm Cove Beach, with its tranquil waters, lush surroundings, and close proximity to some of Australia's most famous natural attractions, is a beautiful, relaxing beach destination offering a perfect balance of comfort and nature.

Top 4 Beautiful Beaches in Australia from West Side

Elliston Beach (Eyre Peninsula, South Australia)

Elliston Beach is a hidden gem located on the Eyre Peninsula in South Australia, offering pristine beaches, clear waters, and dramatic coastal cliffs. The beach is often quiet, providing a peaceful setting for relaxation and beach walks. It’s a great place for swimming, fishing, and exploring the nearby sea caves and rock formations. The colourful sunsets over the ocean add an extra layer of magic to the already breathtaking scenery.

Turquoise Bay (Cape Range National Park, Western Australia)

Turquoise Bay lives up to its name, with crystal-clear, turquoise waters and a pristine, white sandy beach. Located in Cape Range National Park, near Exmouth, this beach is ideal for snorkeling, as it offers easy access to the Ningaloo Reef, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The waters are calm, making it perfect for swimming and spotting vibrant marine life like reef fish, turtles, and rays. The rugged cliffs and surrounding natural beauty further enhance the beach's charm.

Lucky Bay (Cape Le Grand National Park, Western Australia)

Lucky Bay is famous for its powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters. Located in Cape Le Grand National Park, near Esperance, it is often referred to as one of the most beautiful beaches in Australia. It’s also known for the friendly kangaroos that can often be seen lounging on the beach, adding to its charm.

Smiths Beach (Yallingup, Western Australia)

Smiths Beach is a picturesque beach located in the Margaret River region. It is renowned for its pristine environment, ideal surf conditions, and the surrounding natural beauty of the area. It's a popular spot for surfers, but also perfect for swimming, picnicking, or just relaxing with beautiful views of the coastline.

Top 4 Beautiful Beaches in New Zealand from North Side

Ninety Mile Beach (Northland)

Despite its name, Ninety Mile Beach is about 55 miles (88 km) long. It's a remote, stunning stretch of golden sand on the west coast of the North Island, famous for its dramatic sunsets, surf, and as a place for adventure activities like sandboarding. Highlights: Surfing, fishing, and the opportunity to spot dolphins.

Bay of Islands (Northland)

The Bay of Islands is a gorgeous, sheltered area with clear turquoise waters, numerous islands, and secluded beaches. It’s a popular spot for sailing, swimming, and exploring the abundant marine life. Highlights: Swimming with dolphins, snorkeling, sailing, and relaxing on the pristine beaches.

Hot Water Beach (Coromandel Peninsula)

Known for its geothermal activity, Hot Water Beach allows visitors to dig their own hot pool in the sand at low tide. The striking contrast of the hot, mineral-rich water and the cool ocean makes for a unique beach experience. Highlights: Digging your own hot pool, surfing, and exploring the nearby Cathedral Cove.

Raglan (Waikato)

Raglan is a laid-back surf town famous for its long sandy beaches and consistent surf breaks. Its beaches like Manu Bay are world-renowned among surfers, and the surrounding landscape, with lush hills, adds to the charm. Highlights: Surfing, beach walks, and the scenic views of the surrounding hills and ocean.

Abel Tasman is renowned for its golden sandy beaches, clear turquoise waters, and lush native forests. This coastal area is perfect for those seeking a mix of relaxation and adventure, with options for hiking, kayaking, and boat cruises. Wharariki Beach is a hidden gem, known for its dramatic coastal landscape, featuring towering sand dunes, wind-sculpted rock formations, and huge, arching cliffs. Kaiteriteri Beach (Tasman Bay)

Kaiteriteri Beach offers a perfect blend of golden sand, calm blue waters, and the backdrop of rolling hills. It’s a family-friendly beach ideal for swimming, boating, and enjoying the serene atmosphere. Highlights: Swimming, kayaking, and boat tours to nearby islands.

Curio Bay (Catlins Coast)

Curio Bay is famous for its unique blend of natural beauty, including dramatic cliffs, pristine beaches, and fossilized trees. It’s also a place where you can spot wildlife like dolphins and sea lions, making it a nature-lover’s paradise. Highlights: Fossils, wildlife sightings (including Hector’s dolphins), and scenic coastal walks

Top 4 Beautiful Beaches in New Zealand from East Side

Waimarama Beach (Hawke's Bay)

Waimarama Beach is known for its golden sands and pristine waters, with a backdrop of rolling hills and farmland. It's a fantastic spot for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the peaceful surroundings. The beach is also less crowded than some other popular spots, making it ideal for a relaxed experience. Highlights: Swimming, beach walks, and stunning views of the Hawke's Bay coastline.

Muriwai Beach (Auckland)

Muriwai Beach is famous for its dramatic black sand, rugged cliffs, and powerful surf. It's also home to a large colony of gannets, which can be seen nesting on the cliffs. The beach offers a great mix of nature, wildlife, and stunning ocean views. Highlights: Surfing, birdwatching (gannet colony), and breathtaking views from the cliffs.

Mahia Beach (Hawke's Bay)

Mahia Beach offers golden sands, clear waters, and a relaxed atmosphere, making it perfect for swimming, fishing, and sunbathing. The area is less commercialized, which adds to its charm, and it's often less crowded, providing a peaceful beach experience. Highlights: Swimming, fishing, and enjoying the quiet, natural beauty of the Mahia Peninsula.

Castlepoint Beach (Wairarapa)

Castlepoint Beach is a stunning, remote location with white sand, clear blue waters, and dramatic coastal features. The iconic Castle Rock, a rocky outcrop, sits at the entrance to the bay and offers spectacular views of the coastline. It’s a great place for beach walks, swimming, and exploring the nearby lighthouse. Highlights: Castle Rock views, lighthouse, swimming, and hiking along the coastline.

Punakaiki Beach (West Coast)

Punakaiki Beach is set against the backdrop of the famous Pancake Rocks and blowholes. The beach features wild, surf-swept waters and dark sands. The nearby limestone formations create a striking contrast with the beach, making it one of the most scenic spots on the West Coast. Highlights: The Pancake Rocks, blowholes, and dramatic coastline views.

Bethells Beach (Auckland)

Bethells Beach is known for its dramatic black sand and powerful surf. It’s surrounded by lush bushland, and the beach is less commercialized, offering a more serene and natural environment. Westport Beach (West Coast)

Westport Beach offers expansive golden sands and is a great spot for beach walks, bird watching, and surf casting. The beach is wide and uncrowded, giving visitors a sense of peacefulness while still being close to the small town of Westport. The nearby Tauranga Bay is also home to a seal colony. Highlights: Uncrowded golden sands, bird watching, seal colony at Tauranga Bay.

Hokitika Beach (West Coast)
