Ireland

Binevenagh Beach

A long stretch of sandy beach with dramatic cliffs surrounding it, Binevenagh is perfect for nature lovers and those seeking peaceful solitude. The beach is often less crowded, making it ideal for quiet walks and birdwatching.

Benone Strand

Known for its wide, sandy shoreline and views of the Mourne Mountains, Benone is perfect for both families and water sports enthusiasts. It’s also a Blue Flag beach, which ensures cleanliness and safety.

Ballintoy Beach

Famous for its dramatic rock formations and picturesque scenery, Ballintoy Beach has been used as a filming location for Game of Thrones. Its rugged beauty, surrounded by cliffs and the wild Atlantic Ocean, makes it one of the most captivating beaches in Ireland.

Whiterocks Beach

Known for its stunning white limestone cliffs, this beach is popular with surfers, photographers, and nature lovers. The impressive rock formations and caves make Whiterocks an excellent spot for exploration, with panoramic views of the Atlantic and Rathlin Island. These beaches not only showcase Ireland’s natural beauty but also offer a perfect escape into tranquil, scenic surroundings.

Banna Strand

Banna Strand is a wide, sandy beach stretching over 4 kilometers along the Atlantic coast. With its sweeping views of the Slieve Mish Mountains, it’s a favorite for walkers, kite surfers, and those looking to relax by the sea. The beach is also known for its historical significance, as it was the site of the 1916 Banna Strand incident involving Roger Casement.

Garretstown Beach

This pristine, sandy beach is surrounded by rolling dunes and offers crystal-clear waters. It’s popular for swimming, surfing, and beach sports. The beach's sheltered location makes it ideal for a peaceful escape, while the nearby village of Kinsale offers great dining and amenities.

Curracloe Strand

Known for its wide, unspoiled sandy shore and dramatic dunes, Curracloe Strand is one of Ireland’s most famous beaches. It gained international fame as the filming location for Saving Private Ryan. The beach is a great spot for walks, birdwatching, and swimming, with its clear waters and peaceful surroundings.

Tramore Beach

Tramore is a long, sandy beach famous for its great surf and family-friendly atmosphere. The beach has a vibrant, lively vibe with many visitors enjoying walks, water sports, and scenic views of the coastline. The nearby sand dunes and cliffs add to its beauty, making it an excellent spot for a day trip. These beaches on Ireland’s southern coast showcase the country’s stunning natural landscapes, offering perfect places for both relaxation and adventure.

Wicklow’s Silver Strand

This hidden gem is a secluded cove surrounded by cliffs and rolling hills. Silver Strand is known for its peaceful atmosphere, clear waters, and golden sand. It’s a perfect spot for a quiet walk, a swim, or just relaxing with stunning views of the Irish Sea.

Portmarnock Beach

Portmarnock Beach, also known as "The Velvet Strand," is a long, sandy beach with spectacular views of the Howth Peninsula and Lambay Island. This beach is ideal for walking, picnics, and swimming. Its spaciousness makes it great for families, and it is also well-connected to Dublin, making it an easily accessible escape from the city.

Baldoyle Strand

Baldoyle Strand is a beautiful, unspoiled beach with a peaceful atmosphere. It’s a haven for birdwatching, thanks to its proximity to the Baldoyle Bay Nature Reserve. The beach has great walking paths, and its wide, sandy shoreline is ideal for long, scenic walks along the coast.

Curracloe Strand

This stunning beach is a popular destination for visitors to the east coast. Known for its golden sand, dramatic dunes, and clear blue waters, Curracloe Strand is ideal for beach walks, swimming, and picnics. It’s also a well-known filming location for Saving Private Ryan, adding to its historical charm. These east coast beaches provide a range of experiences, from peaceful, secluded coves to lively, easily accessible beaches. Whether you’re looking for a quiet retreat or a fun, active beach day, these spots offer something for everyone.

Inisheer Beach

Inisheer Beach is a serene, sandy spot with clear waters and stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean. The island itself is a haven for nature lovers and offers a glimpse of traditional Irish culture. This beach is perfect for peaceful walks and relaxing by the sea, with the backdrop of historic ruins and scenic landscapes.

Keem Bay

Surrounded by steep cliffs, Keem Bay is one of Ireland's most picturesque beaches. The turquoise waters and sandy shores make it a favorite for swimming, kayaking, and picnicking. The dramatic setting, with its panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean and rugged mountains, makes Keem Bay a truly magical spot.

Spanish Point

This beach is famous for its wide, sandy stretch, perfect for leisurely walks and enjoying the sea breeze. Spanish Point is popular for swimming, surfing, and sailing, and it also offers spectacular views of the Cliffs of Moher on a clear day. The beach has historical significance as well, with ties to the Spanish Armada.

Trá na Fírinne

Trá na Fírinne, or "Beach of Truth," is a secluded and stunning beach with soft sand and clear blue waters. It’s perfect for those looking to escape the crowds and enjoy the beauty of nature. The beach is often less visited, offering tranquility and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and the Atlantic Ocean. These west coast beaches are celebrated for their natural beauty and rugged charm, making them ideal for those seeking both relaxation and adventure in stunning landscapes.
